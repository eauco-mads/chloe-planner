✦ The Four Phases

Your cycle mirrors the seasons.
Each phase has its own intelligence.

Understanding which phase you're in is the foundation of everything. Once you know, the body's language becomes clear.

✦ Winter · Days 1–5 approx
Menstrual Phase
Rest + Reflection + Renewal
Rest Gentle movement Warm foods Minerals Journaling

The body is shedding and renewing. Energy naturally turns inward. This is not a time to push — it's a time to receive. Rest here is productive. Warmth, nourishment, and quiet reflection support what the body is already doing. What needs to be released? What clarity arises in the stillness?

✦ Spring · Days 6–13 approx
Follicular Phase
Growth + Creation + New Beginnings
Creativity Strength training New projects Fresh foods Learning

Estrogen rises and energy returns. This is the beginning of a new cycle — full of creative potential and clarity. The mind feels sharper, initiative comes more naturally, and new beginnings feel genuinely exciting. An excellent time to start things, set intentions, and build physical strength.

✦ Summer · Days 14–17 approx
Ovulatory Phase
Connection + Expression + Peak Energy
Communication Social energy Peak movement Collaboration Confidence

The shortest phase — and often the most electric. Natural charisma and communication are at their peak. Energy is highest. This is the time for visibility: important meetings, hard conversations, collaborations, and putting yourself out there. Your magnetism is genuinely at its most powerful.

✦ Autumn · Days 18–28 approx
Luteal Phase
Grounding + Completion + Inner Work
Boundaries PMS support Organization Nervous system care Slowing down

Progesterone rises, and energy begins to draw inward again. Attention to detail is heightened — excellent for editing, organizing, and completing projects. As the phase progresses, the body needs more support: blood sugar balance, nervous system regulation, and honoring the need to slow down. PMS often lives here — and almost always has a nutritional root.

Before adding more.
Support the foundations.

Most hormone imbalance isn't a mystery — it has roots. Cycle Harmony teaches you where to look first.

🌿

Environment + Endocrine Support

Endocrine disruptors are everywhere — and most people don't know what they're actually exposed to. Small swaps, not fear.

  • Plastics & BPA
  • Synthetic fragrances
  • Conventional cleaning products
  • Non-stick cookware
  • Pesticides on produce
☀️

Circadian Rhythm Support

Light is information. Your hormones are deeply tied to your body clock — and most people are disrupting it daily without realizing it.

  • Morning sunlight exposure
  • Sleep consistency
  • Evening screen use
  • Rest as hormonal support
🫁

Stress + Nervous System

Cortisol and progesterone compete for the same precursor. Chronic stress is a direct hormonal disruptor — and it's also the most overlooked one.

  • Breathwork practices
  • Meditation & rest
  • Journaling for regulation
  • Safety as a hormonal signal
🩸

Blood Sugar Balance

Blood sugar dysregulation is one of the most common and least talked-about drivers of hormone imbalance. It shows up in PMS, cravings, and mid-cycle crashes.

  • Protein + healthy fats first
  • Balanced meal structure
  • Cravings as information
  • Movement after eating

✦ The Bigger Picture

Your hormones don't live
in isolation.

They live inside a body that feels, a nervous system that responds, and a planet that moves in rhythm.

🫀

Hormones Respond to Emotions

Every emotion you feel is a chemical event. Cortisol rises when you feel unsafe. Oxytocin flows when you feel loved. Progesterone drops when you're chronically stressed. Your feelings are not separate from your hormones — they are your hormones. When you dismiss an emotion, you are dismissing chemistry. When you tend to how you feel, you are doing hormonal support work.

🌕

Your Cycle and the Moon

Before artificial light, women's cycles naturally synced with the lunar cycle — bleeding with the new moon, ovulating with the full moon. The same gravitational force that moves tides moves fluid in the body. New moon energy mirrors menstruation: dark, inward, releasing. Full moon mirrors ovulation: peak, illuminated, magnetic. Tracking both reveals a larger rhythm you've always been part of.

🌞

Light, Seasons, and the Body

Morning sunlight sets your circadian clock, which regulates cortisol, melatonin, thyroid function, and ultimately sex hormones. Seasonal light shifts change hormone production across the year — this is why mood, energy, and libido can shift with the seasons. You are not imagining it. You are responding to the same information every living thing on this planet responds to. Your body knows exactly what it's doing.

✦ Questions

Common Questions

Is this for people with irregular cycles too? ▾
Yes — in fact, cycle education is especially valuable when cycles are irregular or difficult to predict. Understanding what your hormones are doing (and what disrupts them) is the first step toward creating more regularity. You don't need a textbook 28-day cycle to benefit from cycle literacy.
What if I'm on hormonal birth control? ▾
Cycle Harmony is still valuable for understanding how hormones work and what your body needs for overall wellbeing. The four-phase model doesn't apply in the same way when on hormonal contraceptives, but the foundations — nutrition, stress, environment, nervous system — are universally relevant. We also cover non-hormonal birth control education in the ebook.
Is this medical advice? ▾
No — Cycle Harmony is educational content only. Nothing here replaces the care of a healthcare provider. We believe that being informed and curious about your own body makes you a better advocate for yourself in medical conversations. Use this knowledge as context, not as diagnosis or treatment.
